<br/>Details at end.</p>It's the 25th! Whatever you celebrate this time of year, I wish you the very best and hope you are having a lovely day. For me, this is a family time: I'm not at all religious but was brought up to celebrate Christmas. So, today we'll be cooking a massive roast dinner and enjoying family time[^teenage].

[^teenage]: As much as our teenage children will tolerate, anyway.

This series was an idea I had around this time last year, and it has been a substantial amount of work. I've really enjoyed writing it, and seeing the impact it has had on the compiled language community. I realise now in retrospect I exclusively used C and C++[^rust], and concentrated on x86 a bit too much. If I do this again, I'll try and widen my horizons!

[^rust]: In my notes I had at least a couple of Rust ideas, but never got around to them.

Speaking of doing it again, I have a number of half-researched, half-written ideas that didn't make the cut. I may either write a few more non-advent posts exploring them, or keep them for another year. Right now I can't promise anything: most of the work for this series was done while I was between jobs, and now I am [working full time](https://www.hudsonrivertrading.com/) I don't know if I'll be able to do this again. Despite the low-budget look of the videos, and the shortness of the posts, it has been a pretty phenomenal amount of work. I don't know how regular content creators do it! I may post a follow-up on "the making of AoCO", and go into some details of how I approached this series.

I'd like to thank a number of people:
